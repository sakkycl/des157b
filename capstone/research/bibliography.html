Capstone Project Research: Annotated Bibliography

“Ark of Taste • Slow Food USA.” Slow Food USA, slowfoodusa.org/ark-of-taste/.

The website opens a discussion about the connection between culture and heritage with endangered foods. The website celebrates countries' biological, cultural, and culinary heritage, encouraging farmers, ranchers, fishers, chefs, grocers, educators, and consumers to protect foods from the dangers of climate change. The website provides two large catalogs of both international and American foods. These catalogs includes information about a variety of foods and their biological, cultural, and traditional backgrounds. The catalog also includes information on how the foods are at risk because of environmental factors. The website and catalogs serves as a place for people all over the world to celebrate these foods and raise awareness.

Finney, Clare. “Endangered Foods: Why Our Diet Is Narrower than Ever - and the UK Urgently Needs to Save These Seven Foods.” The Guardian, 23 Jan. 2023, www.theguardian.com/food/2023/jan/23/endangered-foods-why-our-diet-is-narrower-than-ever-and-these-seven-foods-urgently-need-saving.

The article raises concern over the connection between endangered food issue and the diversity problem in food. The article claims that the number of products available has increased but the diversity of crops has decreases globally, with many products being produced from a limited number of ingredients such as wheat, maize, palm oil, and soy. This raises nutritional concerns such as more people increasing their sugar intake and decreasing their fibre intake. The article also discuses many foods in the UK that are endangered such as cheshire cheese, beremeal, Norfolk beefing apple, perry, saddleback pigs, Scottish birch water, and wild Atlantic salmon.

Shop, gestalten US. “The Museum of Endangered Foods.” Gestalten US Shop, us.gestalten.com/blogs/journal/the-museum-of-endangered-foods.

The article opens the discussion of the connection between food and the climate change that is rarely discussed. The article mentions a variety of foods that are affected by the climate crisis such as avocados, coffee, wine, chocolate, potatoes, chickpeas, fish, and bananas. The article reveals that the decline in the production of these foods are often due to rising temperatures, fresh-water scarcity, irregular weather patterns, habitats loss, deforestation, pollution, and vulnerability to plagues. The article raises concern over the issue because of how it could impact nutrition and traditional diets across the world and advocates for reversing the effects of climate change.
